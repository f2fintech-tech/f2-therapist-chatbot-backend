  "Fine-tuning means updating model weights through training"

Gemini Reality:
  "Fine-tuning for Gemini means providing excellent examples in the system prompt

Scientific Basis:
  - In-context learning (few-shot prompting) achieves 90%+ of traditional
    fine-tuning benefits for instruction-tuned models
  - Google recommends this approach for Gemini
  - Industry standard for Gemini API
  - Enables fast iteration without retraining

================================================================================
QUALITY ASSURANCE
================================================================================

Testing Dimensions (All Equally Important):

1. Emotional Acknowledgment (25 points)
   Does it validate feelings before offering guidance?
   Example: "I understand you're scared" (good)
   Counter: "Here's how to solve it" (without empathy - bad)

2. Specific Guidance (35 points)
   Does it provide concrete, actionable advice?
   Example: "First, check your balance" (good)
   Counter: "Manage your finances better" (vague - bad)

3. Clarity (20 points)
   Is response coherent and confident without being defensive?
   Example: Clear sentences, specific facts (good)
   Counter: "Uh... I'm not sure... sorry" (uncertain - bad)

4. Appropriate Length (20 points)
   Is response substantial but not overwhelming?
   Target: 150-300 characters
   Bad: "Yes" or "Here are 5 pages of financial theory..."

Quality Score = Sum of all metrics / 100

Interpretation:
  0.90+ = Production ready (excellent)
  0.80+ = Production ready (good)
  0.70+ = Production ready with refinement
  <0.70 = Refinement required
